# 04 — Claims Template Review Sheets

**How to use (workshop Part 3):** one sheet per pair, tested against a real historical
claim file. Tick ✔ items the template gets right, cross ✘ items that are wrong or
unnecessary, write in what's missing. Every sheet ends with a feedback block —
complete it even if everything passed. These sheets become template v0.2.

*Terminology note: the "Red flags" sections below are template content and keep that
name. In the assessor-facing product UI the same mechanism is labelled a
**"Concern flag"** — the softer term is what appears on screen.*

Print each sheet separately (sheets are separated by horizontal rules).

---

## Sheet 1 — Geyser / Water Damage

**Reviewer:** ______________________ **Claim file ref:** ______________ **Date:** ________

### Required evidence (capture in every geyser claim)
- ☐ Geyser wide shot showing location (roof cavity / cupboard / external)
- ☐ Rating plate close-up — make, serial, capacity, manufacture date *(high-res photo)*
- ☐ Point of failure if visible (burst seam, element flange, fitting)
- ☐ Drip tray + overflow pipe (presence, routing, condition)
- ☐ Pressure control / safety valve; vacuum breakers
- ☐ Water damage path: ceilings, cornices, walls — wide + close
- ☐ Floor coverings and each affected contents item
- ☐ Panoramic context shot of each affected room

### Optional evidence (capture where relevant)
- ☐ Electrical isolator and water shut-off
- ☐ Replacement geyser already installed (+ its rating plate)
- ☐ Access route to geyser (supports "plate inaccessible" notes)
- ☐ Municipal water meter (leak-rate context in slow-leak disputes)

### Required documents
- ☐ Plumber's report and invoice (emergency + replacement)
- ☐ Quotes for resultant damage (ceiling, paint, flooring)
- ☐ Contents replacement quotes/proof
- ☐ Geyser purchase/installation invoice or CoC, if available

### Key questions the assessor must answer
1. Age of geyser and the evidence source for it (plate / invoice / client estimate)?
2. Sudden burst or gradual leak — how long was water present before action?
3. Installation compliant (drip tray, overflow, valves per SANS 10254)?
4. Who did the emergency work, and was it reasonable?
5. Is the resultant damage consistent with the failure mode and timeline?

### Red flags
- ☐ Long-term staining/mould presented as sudden burst
- ☐ Plate date contradicts stated age; element failure claimed as burst
- ☐ Non-compliant installation contributing to damage
- ☐ Resultant damage implausibly far from the geyser
- ☐ Replacement pre-done at inflated cost, old unit discarded

### The assessor must confirm
- ☐ Identity of person on camera and relationship to policyholder
- ☐ Loss address matches risk address
- ☐ All claimed damage was shown on camera
- ☐ Failure mode finding stated with evidence basis

### Mandatory physical-assessment triggers
- Suspected slow leak with structural implications (saturated walls/foundations)
- Ceiling collapse or unsafe ceiling requiring access inspection
- Resultant damage quantum above agreed threshold (R________ — set in workshop)
- Geyser/plate inaccessible AND age/compliance is decisive for the claim

### What may be handled virtually
Typical geyser burst with visible unit, accessible damage, cooperative client —
**the default claim type for virtual assessment.** Expected virtual-completion rate:
high.

- Items that should be removed/optional: __________________________________________
- Wording/order changes: __________________________________________________________
- Would this template have produced the evidence your historical file needed? ☐ Yes ☐ No — gap: ______________________________
- Which items could NOT have been captured virtually on your file, and why: ________
- Overall verdict: ☐ Use as-is ☐ Use with changes above ☐ Rework needed

## Sheet 2 — Storm Damage

**Reviewer:** ______________________ **Claim file ref:** ______________ **Date:** ________

### Required evidence
- ☐ All affected exterior elevations, wide shots
- ☐ Roof damage from safe vantage points only *(never instruct client onto roof/ladder)*
- ☐ Displaced/broken tiles, sheeting, flashing, ridge capping — as viewable
- ☐ Gutters/downpipes: damage AND pre-existing condition (rust, sagging)
- ☐ Interior ingress points: ceilings, walls, window/door surrounds
- ☐ Each claimed external item: fences, walls, carport, outbuildings, paving
- ☐ Affected contents item by item
- ☐ Panoramic context of each affected area

### Optional evidence
- ☐ Fallen trees/branches and what they struck
- ☐ Temporary repairs in place (tarpaulins)
- ☐ Neighbouring properties' visible storm damage (event corroboration)
- ☐ Client's own photos/videos from the event day (request originals via upload)

### Required documents
- ☐ Repair quotes (roof, ceilings, painting, fencing)
- ☐ Emergency/temporary repair invoices
- ☐ Contents quotes where applicable

### Key questions
1. Storm date/time — corroborated against weather records? (source noted)
2. Damage pattern consistent with the reported wind/hail/rain direction and severity?
3. Pre-storm maintenance condition of the damaged elements?
4. Single event or accumulated prior damage?
5. Are temporary repairs reasonable and invoiced?

### Red flags
- ☐ Rust, rot, perished waterproofing claimed as storm damage
- ☐ Damage on elevations sheltered from reported wind direction
- ☐ Old stains / bubbling paint / mould = long-standing ingress
- ☐ One storm event leveraged into full refurbishment scope
- ☐ No weather event on record for the reported date

### The assessor must confirm
- ☐ Identity + address anchor
- ☐ Storm-attributable vs maintenance-related items separated **item by item**
- ☐ Roof viewing limitations recorded explicitly
- ☐ All claimed damage shown

### Mandatory physical-assessment triggers
- Roof damage material to quantum but not adequately viewable from safe vantage
- Structural damage suspected (cracked walls, shifted roof structure)
- Quantum above threshold (R________)
- Widespread catastrophe event where desktop/drone programme applies instead

### What may be handled virtually
Ground-visible roof damage, fences/walls, internal ingress damage, contents.
Roof-dominant claims are the weak spot — expect frequent "physical recommended for
roof only" hybrid outcomes.

## Sheet 3 — Theft / Burglary

**Reviewer:** ______________________ **Claim file ref:** ______________ **Date:** ________

### Required evidence
- ☐ Point of entry: close-up + context (damaged door/lock/window/bars, tool marks)
- ☐ Broken glass position (inside vs outside fall)
- ☐ Other entry points confirmed secure/undamaged
- ☐ Security measures: burglar bars, gates, alarm panel/keypad, beams, electric fence
- ☐ Each location items were taken from (empty bracket, drawer, safe)
- ☐ Safe close-up if jewellery/firearms claimed (type, mounting, damage)
- ☐ General interior state (ransacked vs targeted)
- ☐ Panoramic context shots

### Optional evidence
- ☐ Alarm company signage/contract shown on camera
- ☐ CCTV equipment and DVR (and whether footage exists)
- ☐ Remaining similar items (lifestyle consistency)
- ☐ SAPS case number document shown on camera *(high-res photo)*

### Required documents
- ☐ SAPS case number confirmation
- ☐ Alarm activation/attendance report (where policy-relevant)
- ☐ Proof of ownership per significant item (receipts, statements, photos in situ, valuations)
- ☐ Itemised claimed list with replacement quotes

### Key questions
1. Forcible and violent entry evident, per policy definition?
2. Policy security warranties met (alarm armed/monitored, bars, safe use)?
3. Timeline: who was where, when discovered, when reported to SAPS?
4. Did armed response attend? Record obtainable?
5. Ownership verification status per major item?

### Red flags
- ☐ No forced entry (mysterious disappearance vs theft cover)
- ☐ Glass broken from inside; entry marks inconsistent with account
- ☐ Alarm unarmed / monitoring lapsed where warranted
- ☐ High-value items recently added; claim soon after inception
- ☐ No ownership proof for most items; items inconsistent with visible lifestyle
- ☐ All receipts same source/date; serials unknown for all electronics
- ☐ Prior similar claims

### The assessor must confirm
- ☐ Identity + address anchor
- ☐ Every policy security warranty: requirement → observed status → evidence ref
- ☐ Entry evidence described factually (observations, not accusations)
- ☐ Ownership verification status listed per major item

### Mandatory physical-assessment triggers
- Entry-method inconsistencies that need hands-on examination (tool marks, locks)
- High-value jewellery/firearms where safe compliance is decisive and not clearly
  demonstrable on camera
- Quantum above threshold (R________)
- Suspected staging — escalate to investigator, do not continue probing on camera

### What may be handled virtually
Entry point verification, security compliance walk-through, item location review,
document collection. Forensic-level entry examination cannot be virtual.

## Sheet 4 — Fire Damage

**Reviewer:** ______________________ **Claim file ref:** ______________ **Date:** ________

### Required evidence
- ☐ Exterior context of property and affected structure
- ☐ Apparent area of origin: widest → closest sequence
- ☐ Burn/smoke patterns as visible (V-patterns, char depth)
- ☐ DB board and any suspect appliance/wiring at origin
- ☐ Room-by-room: structural, contents, smoke/soot spread, heat vs smoke-only zones
- ☐ Remains of significant claimed items (appliance carcasses etc.)
- ☐ Panoramic context per room

### Optional evidence
- ☐ Fire brigade attendance evidence / report number
- ☐ Utilities status (prepaid meter, gas installation)
- ☐ Securing/mitigation measures taken since the fire

### Required documents
- ☐ Fire brigade report (where attended)
- ☐ SAPS case number if opened
- ☐ Electrician's report where electrical cause suspected
- ☐ Contents inventory with ownership proof for significant items
- ☐ Structural repair quotes / QS input for major damage

### Key questions
1. Most probable area of origin and cause **per available evidence** (with confidence qualifier)?
2. Discovery and response timeline; who was present?
3. Damage distribution consistent with described origin/timeline?
4. Occupancy status at the time (occupied/unoccupied/vacant)?
5. Habitable? Alternative accommodation triggered? Property secured?

### Red flags
- ☐ Multiple apparent origin points
- ☐ Burn intensity inconsistent with fuel load
- ☐ Expected household contents absent from debris
- ☐ Recent sum-insured increase / recent inception / property unsold on market
- ☐ Conflicting household accounts of discovery
- ☐ Unexplained fire at vacant/unoccupied property

### The assessor must confirm
- ☐ Identity + address anchor
- ☐ **Scope limitation stated: virtual assessment cannot determine cause definitively**
- ☐ Observations recorded neutrally — indicators flagged, arson never alleged
- ☐ Habitability and mitigation addressed

### Mandatory physical-assessment triggers *(fire is physical-first by default)*
- ANY fire beyond a minor, contained incident (single appliance, single room,
  cause obvious) → physical/forensic investigation
- Cause uncertain or origin unclear → forensic investigator
- Structural involvement → physical + engineer
- Any fraud indicator above → investigator, stop probing on camera

### What may be handled virtually
Minor contained fires with obvious cause; initial triage/scoping of larger fires
(extent overview, habitability, mitigation, document collection) **as a precursor to
physical attendance, not a substitute**.

## Sheet 5 — Accidental Damage

**Reviewer:** ______________________ **Claim file ref:** ______________ **Date:** ________

### Required evidence
- ☐ Damaged item: overall + close-ups from multiple angles
- ☐ Serial/model plate where applicable *(high-res photo)*
- ☐ Incident location with client walking through what happened
- ☐ Panoramic context shot

### Optional evidence
- ☐ Related damage (surface item fell onto, liquid residue)
- ☐ Item partially functioning on camera (powers on, screen state)
- ☐ Photos of item in situ before damage (from client's gallery via upload)

### Required documents
- ☐ Repair quote or BER letter from approved repairer
- ☐ Purchase proof (receipt, statement, packaging with serial)
- ☐ Specified-items schedule extract where relevant

### Key questions
1. Exact circumstances — what, who, when?
2. Damage physically consistent with the described mechanism?
3. Accident vs wear-and-tear/defect/gradual damage?
4. Repair vs beyond-economic-repair, on what quote basis?
5. Correct cover section (specified all-risks vs contents extension)?

### Red flags
- ☐ Damage pattern inconsistent with account (pressure crack on "dropped" screen)
- ☐ Item at end of life; failure dressed as accident
- ☐ Repeat accidental-damage claims on similar items
- ☐ No ownership proof for recently damaged high-value item
- ☐ Damage conveniently timed with upgrade cycles

### The assessor must confirm
- ☐ Identity confirmed
- ☐ Mechanism assessment explicit: account given vs physical indicators observed
- ☐ Repair/replace recommendation with quote reference

### Mandatory physical-assessment triggers
- Item must be physically examined to determine mechanism (rare — specialist bench
  assessment via repairer usually suffices instead)
- Quantum above threshold (R________)

### What may be handled virtually
Almost everything — this should be the fastest template (target: 15–20 minute
session). Keep it proportionate to small claims.

## Sheet 6 — General Non-Motor (catch-all)

**Reviewer:** ______________________ **Claim file ref:** ______________ **Date:** ________
**Peril on your test file:** ______________________ *(power surge, burst pipe, impact,
malicious damage, glass, other)*

### Required evidence
- ☐ Context shots of property/affected area
- ☐ Close-ups of all claimed damage/items
- ☐ Cause-related evidence appropriate to the peril, e.g.:
  - Power surge: DB board, surge protection presence, each affected appliance + rating plate
  - Burst pipe: pipe section, access point, water path
  - Impact: the impacting object/vehicle evidence, structure damage
- ☐ Panoramic context per affected area

### Optional evidence
- ☐ Documents shown on camera (or flagged for upload)
- ☐ Third-party evidence where applicable (vehicle details, SAPS accident ref)

### Required documents
- ☐ Cause-supporting report (electrician/plumber/technician as applicable)
- ☐ Repair/replacement quotes
- ☐ Ownership proof for items
- ☐ Third-party details where relevant

### Key questions
1. What actually caused the loss — is it an insured peril? (explicit cause finding)
2. Date of loss and discovery; account consistent with evidence?
3. Pre-existing condition/maintenance contribution?
4. Full extent and quantum basis?
5. Any peril-specific policy conditions, and compliance status?

### Red flags
- ☐ Cause vague or shifting between tellings
- ☐ Damage inconsistent with the named peril
- ☐ Accumulated damage claimed as a single event
- ☐ Round-number quotes, single source, all dated after the fact

### The assessor must confirm
- ☐ Identity + address anchor
- ☐ Explicit cause-of-loss finding with evidence basis
- ☐ Policy-relevance comment WITHOUT making the claims decision

### Mandatory physical-assessment triggers
- Peril cannot be identified from what's shown
- Structural involvement
- Quantum above threshold (R________)

### What may be handled virtually
Most contained household perils. This template's weakness is lack of specialised
structure — flag during workshop which recurring perils deserve their own template
next (candidates: power surge, burst pipes).
