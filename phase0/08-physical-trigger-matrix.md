# 08 — Physical Assessment Trigger Matrix

When the assessor should **stop or limit** the virtual assessment and recommend
physical inspection. "Recommend physical" is a first-class, respectable outcome — the
tool's credibility depends on knowing its limits. The evidence captured before the
stop always remains part of the file (virtual triage still adds value).

**Actions legend:**
- **CONTINUE+NOTE** — complete virtually, record the limitation in the report
- **HYBRID** — complete what is capturable virtually; recommend physical for the named element only
- **STOP→PHYSICAL** — wrap up courteously; recommend full physical assessment
- **STOP→ESCALATE** — wrap up courteously; escalate to specialist/investigator; do NOT probe further on camera

| # | Trigger | Detect by | Action | Notes |
|---|---------|-----------|--------|-------|
| T1 | **Poor visibility** — lighting, camera quality, or framing too poor to evidence the key items | Repeated illegible captures of decisive items (plates, entry damage) after torch/high-res attempts | CONTINUE+NOTE if peripheral; HYBRID if decisive items affected | Try: torch, high-res photo request, move closer, daylight reschedule — before triggering |
| T2 | **Poor signal** — video unusable, drops repeatedly | ≥3 disconnects, or sustained quality too low to capture | Reschedule once (different time/place on the property); second failure → STOP→PHYSICAL or upload-link-only route | Partial captures retained; treat as attempt in the no-show/retry flow |
| T3 | **Roof / structural concerns** — roof damage material to quantum but not safely viewable; cracks, movement, collapse risk | Client cannot show the element from any safe vantage; structural indicators on camera | HYBRID (roof/structure physical, rest virtual) | **Never** instruct client onto roofs or ladders — standing safety rule, logged in every session |
| T4 | **Fire origin uncertainty** — cause unclear or fire beyond minor/contained | Anything beyond single-appliance/single-room with obvious cause | STOP→PHYSICAL (forensic) | Virtual session still useful for extent triage, habitability, mitigation — but as precursor, not substitute |
| T5 | **Fraud / inconsistency indicators** — account conflicts with evidence, staging signs, coached answers, off-camera prompting | Concern flags accumulating across checklist items | STOP→ESCALATE (investigator) | Assessor stays neutral on camera; never confronts; report records observations factually |
| T6 | **Large quantum** — claim value above the virtual threshold | Quantum emerging during session exceeds R________ *(set in workshop — suggest separate thresholds for contents vs buildings)* | HYBRID or STOP→PHYSICAL per claim type | Virtual remains fine for verifying existence/context even on big claims — physical validates quantum |
| T7 | **Safety concerns** — client would need to enter unsafe areas (fire-damaged structure, collapsed ceiling, electrical hazards, unstable walls) | Assessor judgement on camera | CONTINUE+NOTE (assess from safe distance) or HYBRID | Client safety overrides evidence completeness, always; instruct explicitly and log the instruction |
| T8 | **Commercial risk complexity** — multi-tenant, hazardous processes, sprinkler/detection systems needing hands-on verification, heavy industrial | Known at job creation (route physical from the start) or discovered in session | STOP→PHYSICAL for the survey/assessment; virtual output becomes a pre-visit briefing | Commercial boundary to be set by surveyors in workshop (sheet S2) |
| T9 | **Client unable/unwilling to show key areas** — mobility, refusal, third party obstructing, "no access" to decisive items | Repeated declines or inability on decisive checklist items | If inability: HYBRID/STOP→PHYSICAL with empathy. If refusal pattern: STOP→ESCALATE | Distinguish *can't* from *won't* in the record — factually, not accusatorially |
| T10 | **Wrong participant** — person on camera is not the insured/authorised representative | Identity confirmation fails at session open | Reschedule with correct participant; log attempt | Do not proceed "just to save the appointment" |

## Rules of use

1. **Triggers are prompts, not shackles.** The assessor's professional judgement
   rules; the matrix standardises when judgement must be exercised and recorded.
2. **Every triggered stop is recorded** on the checklist item(s) concerned, appears
   automatically in the report's Limitations section, and sets the job outcome to
   *escalated to physical* where applicable.
3. **The client never hears trigger language.** On camera it is always: "Thanks —
   for this one we'll arrange for someone to come out and look in person."
