# 03 — Assessor Validation Workshop Guide

**Duration:** 90 minutes (minimum 60 — cut Part 4 first if short)
**Attendees:** 3–5 working assessors/surveyors, 1 claims coordinator, 1 manager/
reviewer, 1 facilitator, 1 note-taker. Small on purpose — this is a working session,
not a presentation.
**Materials:** printed review sheets (doc 04, one set per assessor), UX walkthroughs
(docs 06–07), trigger matrix (doc 08), prototype report structure (doc 09),
**3–5 closed historical claim files** (one per template under test — pulled in advance,
anonymised where needed), pens, and a whiteboard.

**Goal:** validate that the workflow and templates match real assessment practice —
and capture the corrections where they don't.

---

## Agenda

### Part 1 — Frame the concept (10 min)
Facilitator explains in plain terms: guided video assessment, checklist-driven capture,
labelled evidence, generated report. State clearly:
- "The Inspector" is a codename; branding comes later.
- Login/security is deliberately parked; today is about workflow and templates.
- Nothing is built yet — their corrections are cheap now and expensive later.

### Part 2 — Live workflow walk-through (20 min)
Walk the assessor journey (doc 06) and client journey (doc 07) step by step on
printouts/slides. Pause at each step and ask the questions below. Note-taker records
every "that's not how it works" verbatim.

**Questions to ask:**
1. Walk us through your last three assessments — where does this flow differ from
   what you actually did?
2. At what point in a real conversation do you decide what to photograph? Does
   "select checklist item → capture" match that, or do you shoot first and organise
   later? *(If the latter dominates, the unfiled-capture tray becomes primary — we
   need to know.)*
3. What do you do today when the client can't show you something? How often?
4. How do you handle a client who is elderly, panicked, or has a poor phone?
5. When would you refuse to do this virtually at all? *(Feeds Part 5.)*
6. What does the claims handler complain about most in reports you submit today?
7. What would make you personally choose this tool over a WhatsApp video call?
   What would make you quietly stop using it?

### Part 3 — Template validation against historical files (30 min) — the core
Split into pairs. Each pair takes one historical claim file + the matching review
sheet (doc 04) and **re-runs the claim on paper against the template**:

1. Read the file's actual evidence and report.
2. Tick each template item the file needed; cross out items that were irrelevant;
   write in anything the real claim required that the template lacks.
3. Answer on the sheet: *Would this template, followed on a video call, have produced
   the evidence this file actually needed?* If no — what's missing?
4. Mark which items on the sheet could NOT have been captured virtually for this
   specific claim, and why.
5. Complete the feedback block on the sheet.

Pairs report back 3 minutes each. Facilitator collects all sheets.

### Part 4 — Report and evidence pack review (15 min)
Hand out the prototype report structure (doc 09). Questions:
1. Would you sign this report? What's missing before your name goes on it?
2. Would the insurer/claims handler accept it? What would bounce?
3. Is the evidence index useful or clutter?
4. Quantum: should the report carry figures/reserve comment, or observations only?
   *(Feeds decision D-06.)*

### Part 5 — Physical-assessment triggers (10 min)
Review the trigger matrix (doc 08) line by line: agree / amend / add. Push for
concrete thresholds ("large quantum" = what rand value? — feeds the matrix and
decision register).

### Part 6 — Wrap-up and decisions (5 min)
Confirm what was decided, what goes to management, and who reviews the revised
templates (name a **template owner** per claim type before people leave the room).

---

## What feedback we need (note-taker's checklist)

- Per template: missing items, unnecessary items, wrong ordering/grouping, wrong
  wording (must read like an assessor talks, not like a policy document).
- The real capture behaviour: checklist-first vs shoot-first ratio among attendees.
- Frequency estimates: no-shows, missing evidence, "couldn't do this virtually".
- Report credibility gaps.
- Named thresholds for physical-assessment triggers.
- Verbatim objections — especially the unspoken-adoption killers from Q7.

## Decisions that must come out of the session

| # | Decision | Feeds |
|---|----------|-------|
| W1 | Which claim types go into the first prototype (recommend: geyser/water + one other) | Decision register D-02 |
| W2 | Template owner per claim type (named person) | D-08 |
| W3 | Physical-assessment trigger list agreed, with quantum threshold | Doc 08 final |
| W4 | Report carries quantum figures or observations only | D-06 |
| W5 | Are surveys in or out of the first prototype | D-03 |
| W6 | Pilot assessor group volunteers (2–3 names) | D-09 |
| W7 | Capture interaction primary mode (checklist-first confirmed, or tray-first redesign needed) | Doc 06 / build spec |

## After the workshop (facilitator, within 1 week)

1. Update the six review sheets into template v0.2 with all accepted changes.
2. Send template v0.2 to the named owners for sign-off (async, 1 week deadline).
3. Update the trigger matrix and report structure.
4. Deliver the decision inputs (W1–W7) into the decision register (doc 10).
5. Book a 30-minute follow-up only if owners raise conflicts.
