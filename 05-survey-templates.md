# 05 — Risk Survey Templates

Risk surveys use the classic **COPE** underwriting framework (Construction, Occupancy,
Protection, Exposure) extended with Maintenance, Security and Fire risk, ending in
Recommendations and a Risk Grading. Two seed templates: residential and
commercial/property. Both follow the same section skeleton; the commercial version
goes deeper on occupancy/process and fire protection.

## 5.1 Common survey mechanics

- Every section has: guided capture prompts (what the surveyor asks the client to
  show), structured questions, and free observations.
- Every adverse observation can be converted into a **recommendation** with:
  category (Requirement — condition of cover / Improvement — advisory), description,
  risk addressed, and due timeline (immediate / 30 days / 90 days / next renewal).
- The survey concludes with a **risk grading** and an overall comment.

**Risk grading scale (proposal):**

| Grade | Meaning | Typical action |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| A — Excellent | Well constructed, maintained and protected; no material recommendations | Standard terms |
| B — Good | Minor improvements advised | Standard terms + advisories |
| C — Acceptable | Requirements needed to reach acceptable level | Terms subject to compliance within timeline |
| D — Poor | Material deficiencies; significant requirements | Refer to underwriter; loaded terms/conditions |
| E — Unacceptable | Risk outside appetite as presented | Refer/decline recommendation |

Grading is the surveyor's professional judgement supported by section findings — the
tool must display section summaries next to the grading field but must NOT auto-compute
the grade in MVP.

## 5.2 Residential risk survey

### 1. Construction
- **Capture:** all elevations; roof covering as visible; wall close-up; any thatch,
  timber or non-standard elements; outbuildings; lapas/thatch gazebos with distance
  from main dwelling.
- **Questions:** wall construction (brick/block/timber/other); roof (tile/sheet/thatch/
  slate/concrete); approximate age of dwelling; any additions/alterations and were they
  approved; non-standard construction percentage estimate.
- **Watch for:** thatch (distance to dwelling, lightning protection, fire retardant
  treatment), unapproved structures, asbestos roofing.

### 2. Occupancy
- **Questions:** owner-occupied / tenanted / holiday home; periods unoccupied
  (>30/60 days); anyone working from home (business use — stock, clients visiting,
  equipment); number of occupants; domestic staff.
- **Watch for:** undisclosed business use, extended unoccupancy, subletting/short-term
  letting (Airbnb).

### 3. Protection (fire & water)
- **Capture:** geyser installation (drip tray, valves — same standard as claims
  template), electrical DB board (label, condition, earth leakage present), fire
  extinguisher/blanket if any, gas installations (bottle location, certificate),
  fireplace/braai/flue condition.
- **Questions:** earth leakage protection present; gas certificate of conformity held;
  solar/inverter/battery installation — installer certificate, location, ventilation;
  lightning protection (esp. thatch).
- **Watch for:** DIY electrical work, overloaded boards, inverters in living areas or
  poorly ventilated cupboards, LP gas bottles indoors/oversized.

### 4. Exposure
- **Capture:** street view from the gate; neighbouring properties/boundaries; slopes,
  rivers/streams, large trees over structures, veld/bush interface.
- **Questions:** flood history of the property/street; proximity to open veld
  (wildfire), rivers, dams; large trees threatening structures; crime profile of the
  area as experienced.
- **Watch for:** properties below road level (stormwater), retaining walls in poor
  condition, veld boundary without firebreak.

### 5. Maintenance
- **Capture:** gutters/fascias, visible waterproofing, boundary walls (cracks/lean),
  paving, pool area, general upkeep indicators.
- **Questions:** roof last inspected/waterproofed; known damp problems; cracks
  monitored; pool fence/net (liability).
- **Watch for:** deferred maintenance patterns — these become "storm damage" claims.

### 6. Security
- **Capture:** perimeter (walls, fencing, electric fence + energizer certificate),
  gates/motors, external doors and locks, burglar bars, security gates, alarm panel,
  beams, CCTV, safe (type and mounting).
- **Questions:** alarm — monitored? armed response contract? linked to which zones;
  compliance with any policy security requirements; jewellery/firearms — safe
  specification (SABS category for firearms); electric fence certificate of compliance.
- **Watch for:** alarm not in use ("we never arm it"), gaps between policy security
  warranties and reality — this is the single most valuable output of a residential
  survey.

### 7. Fire risk
- Summarised from Construction + Protection: ignition sources (thatch, fireplaces,
  gas, electrical, inverters), detection (smoke detectors rare in SA homes — note if
  present), suppression (extinguishers, hose/pool proximity), separation of
  outbuildings.

### 8. Recommendations & grading
- Structured list of Requirements vs Improvements with timelines (per 5.1).
- Risk grade A–E with overall comment and any items requiring underwriter referral.

## 5.3 Commercial / property risk survey

Same skeleton, deeper content. Aimed at small-to-mid commercial risks assessable
virtually (offices, retail, light industrial, body corporates); heavy industrial risks
should be flagged for physical survey.

### 1. Construction
- **Capture:** all elevations; roof structure/covering from accessible vantage;
  fire walls/separations between units; mezzanines; yard structures; signage/awnings.
- **Questions:** construction class of walls/roof/floors; building age; storeys;
  divisions/tenancies within the building and separation between them; approved
  building plans for alterations.
- **Watch for:** combustible construction (timber, sandwich panel/insulated panels —
  identify core material if possible), unprotected structural steel in fire-loaded
  areas, mixed construction in older buildings.

### 2. Occupancy
- **Capture:** each occupancy area in walk-through: production/process areas, storage
  (commodity types, stacking height, aisle spacing), kitchens/canteens, hazardous
  processes (welding, spraying, charging stations).
- **Questions:** exact business activities on premises; all tenants and their
  activities (multi-tenant); combustible/hazardous materials stored (types,
  quantities, storage method — flammable liquids cabinets, gas storage); stock values
  and seasonal peaks; hours of operation; overnight occupancy.
- **Watch for:** undisclosed hazardous processes, plastics storage, pallets stacked
  against buildings, incompatible tenants (e.g. panel beater under offices),
  battery/solar storage and charging areas.

### 3. Protection
- **Capture:** fire extinguishers (count, service dates on labels), hose reels and
  hydrants, sprinkler control valves if any, fire detection panel, emergency exits and
  signage, DB boards, standby generators/inverter rooms.
- **Questions:** extinguisher/hose reel service current (SANS 1475 service records);
  detection — type and coverage, monitored where; sprinklers — coverage, last
  inspection; fire team/evacuation drills; electrical certificate of compliance;
  thermographic scan of DBs ever done; generator/fuel storage arrangements.
- **Watch for:** expired service labels, blocked exits/hose reels, detection panels in
  fault, storage encroaching sprinkler clearance, non-compliant fuel storage.

### 4. Exposure
- **Capture:** neighbouring buildings/activities on each boundary with separation
  distances; shared walls; street context.
- **Questions:** neighbouring occupancies and their hazard (fuel station, timber yard,
  informal settlement proximity, veld); shared services/walls; flood/stormwater
  history; protest route/civil commotion exposure relevant to location (SASRIA
  context noted, not underwritten here).
- **Watch for:** high-hazard neighbours with inadequate separation, common roof voids
  across tenancies.

### 5. Maintenance & housekeeping
- **Capture:** general housekeeping in work and storage areas, waste management
  (skips location relative to building), yard condition, roof drainage.
- **Questions:** planned maintenance regime; housekeeping responsibility; smoking
  policy and designated areas; hot-work permit system in place.
- **Watch for:** waste accumulation against buildings, poor housekeeping in high-fire-
  load areas — the strongest single predictor of commercial fire severity.

### 6. Security
- **Capture:** perimeter, access control, guarding post, alarm/CCTV coverage, stock
  security (cages, safes for cash/high-value stock), delivery/loading areas.
- **Questions:** guarding (24h? armed response?), alarm monitoring, CCTV recording
  retention, cash handling procedures and limits, high-value stock storage compliance
  with policy warranties, key control.
- **Watch for:** warranty-reality gaps (again the key output), unmonitored after-hours
  periods, staff access to high-value stock uncontrolled.

### 7. Fire risk summary
- Fire load vs protection adequacy; single biggest fire scenario ("estimated maximum
  loss" narrative, qualitative in virtual surveys); business interruption
  vulnerability comment (single premises? key machine? restock lead times?).

### 8. Recommendations & grading
- Requirements vs Improvements with timelines; risk grade A–E; explicit statement of
  virtual-survey limitations and whether a physical survey is recommended (mandatory
  question: *"Is this risk adequately surveyable virtually?"* — No routes it to a
  physical survey recommendation).

## 5.4 Survey report differences from claims

- No claim/loss narrative; instead risk description + section findings.
- Recommendations register is the actionable core — it must export cleanly (table in
  PDF) because underwriters turn these into policy conditions.
- Re-survey/follow-up: recommendations carry status (open/complied/waived) — MVP just
  records them; tracking compliance over time is Phase 4+.
